# Wrap & Shout-Outs
## Closing 20 minutes (15:40–16:00)

No CTF. No prizes. Just the room sharing what surprised them, then
the pointer to what comes next.

---

## Tier check-in (2 min)

> "Before we close: how far did you get today?"

Quick show of hands:
- 🔵 Hunter Lab 1 complete and you stayed there (and that's fine)
- 🔵 Hunter Labs 1–3 complete
- 🔵 Hunter all the way through Module 5 dossier
- 🟠 Ghost graduation from any module
- 🟠 Ghost all the way through, 3 deliverables (MD + KML + JSON)

No judgment. Every row above is a real outcome of a 5-hour session.
The dossier matters more than the tier.

---

## Shout-outs (10 min)

> "What surprised you today?"

Open the floor. Call on whoever raises a hand. Aim for 2–3 voices
per module, distributed across tiers. Examples of what makes a good
shout-out:

- **"I found a weird subdomain"** — have them read the subdomain out
  loud, describe why it's interesting, rate it Admiralty-style. This
  is the highest-value shout-out format.
- **"recon-ng module X totally failed and here's why"** — the
  bit-rot audit is a public good.
- **"The TXT record vendor map had [vendor] I didn't expect"** —
  vendor surprises are often the highest-leverage findings of the
  day.
- **"I tried to script [X] and here's what worked / broke"** —
  scripting shout-outs frequently turn into follow-up contributions
  to the toolkit repo.
- **"My KML screenshot looked like [this]"** — have them show the
  laptop to the room if possible.

Keep each shout-out to ~60 seconds. Cut politely if someone runs long.

---

## The dossier review prompt (3 min)

> "Open your dossier. Read Section 1 — your Executive Summary. Now
> close the laptop. What would you tell your best friend, out loud,
> in 30 seconds, about what you found on Caterpillar today?"

Let it sit for a moment. This is where the workshop becomes **yours,
not ours**. The dossier is a portfolio artifact. The 30-second version
is the elevator pitch version. Both matter.

Follow up:

> "If you can't condense your own dossier into 30 seconds, either
> the dossier is too long, or Section 1 isn't sharp enough. Fix it
> this week. Share it with a colleague. Ask them to pick one finding
> that surprised them — that's your lead sentence for next time."

## What comes next (3 min)

> "Three pointers, in order of investment."

**1. The Jobs Pipeline (zero investment).**

Open `Student-References/Jobs-Pipeline.md` on your laptop or phone.
Pick one row that matches where you want to be in 18 months. Set a
calendar reminder for one week from today to take **one concrete
step** toward that role — a CV update, a portfolio entry, a LinkedIn
message to someone already in that role, a job board application.

That's it. One row. One step. One week.

**2. Re-run today's workflow on a different target.**

Pick a target that matters to you — a company you interview with next
week, a vendor your employer uses, a Fortune 500 you're curious
about. Re-run the 5-module workflow on the new target. The second
run is 3x faster than today and produces a dossier you can actually
use at your job.

Save every dossier you produce. This becomes your portfolio.

**3. Come back and teach this.**

If you want to be a professional at this, **the best thing you can do
is teach it.** OSINT Village runs at every BSides. Call for papers
are open. If you want to propose a session for BSides KC 2027 or
any other conference, the organizer of this workshop will help you —
there are contact details in the `README.md`.
